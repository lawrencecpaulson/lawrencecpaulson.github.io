A set is hereditarily finite if it is finite and all of its elements are hereditarily finite. They can be characterised by the axioms of set theory but negating the axiom of infinity. They are countably many and they are a natural domain for talking about computation. They also allow a clean treatment of GÃ¶del's incompleteness theorems.
